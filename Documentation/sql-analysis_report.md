
# SQL Investigation Report

## Objective
Use SQL to identify the key drivers of customer churn and provide business recommendations.

---

## Investigation 1: Overall Churn

**Business Question**
How serious is the churn problem?

**Finding**
26.5% of customers have churned, indicating that approximately one in four customers leave the company.

**Recommendation**
Identify the primary churn drivers and prioritize customer retention initiatives.

---

## Investigation 2: Contract Type

**Business Question**
Does contract type influence churn?

**Finding**
Customers on month-to-month contracts have the highest churn rate (42.7%), while two-year contracts have the lowest (2.8%).

**Recommendation**
Encourage customers to switch to long-term contracts through discounts and loyalty benefits.

---

## Investigation 3: Customer Tenure

**Business Question**
Does tenure affect churn?

**Finding**
Customers within their first year have the highest churn rate (47.4%). Churn decreases significantly as tenure increases.

**Recommendation**
Strengthen onboarding and engagement during the first 12 months.

---

## Investigation 4: Internet Service

**Business Question**
Does internet service type influence churn?

**Finding**
Fiber Optic customers experience the highest churn rate (41.9%).

**Recommendation**
Review Fiber pricing, service quality, and customer satisfaction.

---

## Investigation 5: Tech Support

**Business Question**
Does Tech Support reduce churn?

**Finding**
Customers without Tech Support churn nearly three times more than customers with Tech Support.

**Recommendation**
Bundle Tech Support into premium plans or offer free trials.

---

## Investigation 6: Online Security

**Business Question**
Does Online Security influence churn?

**Finding**
Customers without Online Security have significantly higher churn rates.

**Recommendation**
Promote Online Security through bundled plans and promotional offers.

---

## Investigation 7: Payment Method

**Business Question**
Does payment method affect churn?

**Finding**
Customers using Electronic Check have the highest churn rate (45.3%).

**Recommendation**
Investigate customer behavior within this segment and encourage migration to automatic payment methods.

---

# Key Business Findings

- New customers (0–12 months) are the highest-risk segment.
- Month-to-month contracts have the highest churn.
- Fiber Optic customers churn more than DSL customers.
- Customers without Tech Support or Online Security are significantly more likely to leave.
- Electronic Check users have the highest churn among payment methods.

---

# Business Impact

The analysis identified the primary drivers of churn and highlighted customer segments that should be prioritized for retention campaigns, pricing improvements, and service enhancements.
