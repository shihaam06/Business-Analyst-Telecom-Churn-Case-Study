# Business Recommendations

## Executive Summary

Based on the SQL analysis of 7,043 customer records, six key drivers of customer churn were identified. The recommendations below prioritize initiatives that are expected to improve customer retention.

---

## Recommendation 1: Convert Month-to-Month Customers

**Problem**
Month-to-month customers have a churn rate of 42.7%.

**Recommendation**
Offer discounts, loyalty rewards, or exclusive benefits for customers switching to annual or two-year contracts.

**Expected Impact**
Improve customer retention and increase customer lifetime value.

---

## Recommendation 2: Improve First-Year Customer Experience

**Problem**
Customers with tenure below 12 months show the highest churn (47.4%).

**Recommendation**
Launch structured onboarding, welcome campaigns, and proactive follow-ups during the first year.

**Expected Impact**
Reduce early customer attrition.

---

## Recommendation 3: Improve Fiber Optic Experience

**Problem**
Fiber Optic customers churn significantly more than DSL customers.

**Recommendation**
Review pricing, service reliability, and customer complaints for Fiber plans.

**Expected Impact**
Increase satisfaction among premium customers.

---

## Recommendation 4: Bundle Value-Added Services

**Problem**
Customers without Tech Support and Online Security churn at much higher rates.

**Recommendation**
Bundle these services into premium plans or provide free trial periods.

**Expected Impact**
Increase service adoption and improve retention.

---

## Recommendation 5: Review Electronic Check Customers

**Problem**
Electronic Check users have the highest churn among all payment methods.

**Recommendation**
Analyze this customer segment further and encourage migration to automated payment methods.

**Expected Impact**
Reduce churn within a high-risk payment segment.

---

## Expected Business Impact

Implementing these recommendations is expected to improve customer retention, increase customer lifetime value, reduce acquisition costs, and strengthen long-term revenue growth.
